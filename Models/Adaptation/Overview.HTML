A disturbance is initially propagated from input (I) to output (O). As time passes, the output gradually returns to its previous value.

Adaptation is defined by

dA/dt = K * O
O = I - A

The first equation can also be expressed as

A = K * O dt

K is the rate constant of the time delay. The time constant (Tau) of the delay is the reciprocal of the rate constant.

Tau = 1 / K

For a step change in input, when time = Tau, O = 0.63 * I.

The half-time of the step response is the time required for the output go one-half way to its final value.

t 1/2 = 0.69 * Tau
