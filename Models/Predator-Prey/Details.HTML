Here is a list of the Predator-Prey Model's variables.

X is the model's independent variable. It represents time in years. We typically run a solution of 50 or 100 years.

First the moose.

Moose Count is the moose herd size or head count. Units are Number Of Animals.

Moose Change is the change with time of the moose count. Change is the birth rate minus the death rate. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Moose Birth Rate is the birth rate for the moose herd. Birth rate is a function of moose fertility and herd size. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Moose Fertility is a parameter. Units are 1/Year.

Moose Death Rate is the death rate for the moose herd. Death rate is a function of moose mortality and herd size. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Moose Mortality is a function of wolf hunting skills and wolf herd size. Units are 1/Year.

Moose Food Value is a parameter that defines the amount of benefit a wolf gets from each moose killed. Units are 1/Number Of Animals.

Now the wolf.

Wolf Count is the wolf herd size or head count. Units are Number Of Animals.

Wolf Change is the change with time of the wolf count. Change is the birth rate minus the death rate. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Wolf Birth Rate is the birth rate for the wolf herd. Birth rate is a function of wolf fertility and herd size. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Wolf Fertility is a function of the moose food value, the wolf hunting skills and the size of the moose herd. Units are 1/Year.

Wolf Death Rate is the death rate for the wolf herd. Death rate is a function of wolf mortality and herd size. Units are Number Of Animals/Year.

Wolf Mortality is a parameter. Units are 1/Year.

Wolf Hunting Skills is a parameter that defines the amount of benefit a wolf gets from each moose killed. Units are 1/Number Of Animals.
