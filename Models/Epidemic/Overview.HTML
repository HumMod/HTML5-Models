Overview

This is the Epidemic Model. The model shows how an epidemic can grow to involve an entire population, while beginning with a small number of infected individuals.

The population consists of three types of individuals

  • Individuals not infected and never infected. This is the healthy group.

  • Individuals now infected. This is the contagious group.

  • Individuals who were infected and who are now immune or dead. This is the recovered group.

Here are the basic rules

  • Individuals in the contagious group can infect individuals in the healthy group but not individuals in the recovered group.

  • When healthy individuals are infected, they transition to the contagious group.

  • Individuals in the contagious group gradually transition to the recovered group.

The contagion rate is a function of virulence, the contagious head count and the healthy head count.

The recovery rate is a function of the contagious head count.
