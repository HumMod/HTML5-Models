We're shooting the shotput. The idea here is to find the angle (from horizontal) that makes the shotput go the greatest distance -- at a constant initial velocity.

Distance is the integral of velocity over time while velocity is the integral of acceleration over time.

This model is three-dimensional. Acceleration, velocity and distance are described using vertical and horizontal components. The third dimension is time.

There is a little bit of trigonometry involved in that we must find the initial values for vertical and horizontal velocity as a function of the shotput initial velocity and angle (from the horizontal).

The horizontal acceleration is zero. The vertical acceleration is gravity, or -9.8 m/sec2 at the earth's surface.
