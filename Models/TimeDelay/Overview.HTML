Overview

A disturbance is not initially propagated from input to output. As time passes, the output gradually increases to equal the input.

A time delay is defined by

dOutput/dt = K * (Input - Output)

K is the rate constant of the time delay. The time constant (Tau) of the delay is the reciprocal of the rate constant.

Tau = 1 / K

For a step change in input, when time = Tau, Output = 0.63 * Input.

The half-time of the step response is the time required for the output go one-half way to its final value.

t 1/2 = 0.69 * Tau
