Overview

This is the Windkessel Model. The model demonstrates the generation of pulsatile arterial pressure in response to pulsatile ventricular outflow.

The Blood Vessel. The model is build around an elastic arterial compartment - the Windkessel. The compartment has a compliance (C) and an unstressed volume (V0). It generates blood pressure in response to blood volume changes.

The term Windkessel is German. Its means roughly air container. The important concept here is that it is the elastic properties of an artery that determine how much pressure a given volume will generate.

Blood Flow. The Windkessel receives blood flow from the ventricle through an aortic valve and in turn delivers blood flow to the periphery through a vascular conductance.

Blood flows at any instant in time are equal to the product of the pressure gradient and vascular conductance.

Blood Volume. Blood volume is the integral over time of the blood inflow from the ventricle minus the blood outflow to the periphery. The blood volume is calculated using a differential equation.
