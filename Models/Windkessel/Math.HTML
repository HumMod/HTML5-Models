Math

The Windkessel Model has one differential equation that states that the Windkessel volume (Volume) is the integral over time (X) of the change in volume (Change).

(1) Volume = (Change) dX

Note that the change variable is the derivative of volume with respect to the indepedent variable or dVolume/dX.

The volume's rate of change (Change) is equal to the rate of inflow (Inflow) minus the rate of outflow (Outflow).

(2) Change = Inflow - Outflow

Ventricular pressure (VP) has a maximum value (VPMax) and is the positive part of a sine function of time (X). Periodicity is determined by heart rate (HR).

(3) Arg = 2 * Pi * (HR / 60) * X
(4) VP = positive VPMax * sine (Arg)

Windkessel (aortic) pressure (Pressure) is a function of the Windkessel's blood volume (Volume), vascular compliance (C) and unstressed volume (V0).

(5) Pressure = (1 / C) * (Volume - V0)

The Windkessel's rate of blood inflow (Inflow) is equal to the valve conductance (VC) multiplied by the pressure gradient. The pressure gradient is ventricular pressure (VP) minus Windkessel pressure (Pressure). Back flow is not allowed in this initial rendering.

(6) Inflow = VC * (VP - Pressure)

The Windkessel's rate of blood outflow (Outflow) is equal to the peripheral conductance (PC) multiplied by the pressure gradient. The pressure gradient is the Windkessel pressure (Pressure) minus the peripheral pressure (PP).

(7) Outflow = PC * (Pressure - PP)
