Overview

A pendulum accelerates downward gaining momentum. After it passes the bottom, it loses momentum and slows, then it stops and reverses direstion.

Of interest: A pendulum oscillates at a regular interval.

Since the trajectory of a pendulum is restricted to an arc on a circle, use of polar coordinates is appropriate.

Acceleration for this pendulum in the y direction is gravity. The length of the pendulum influences its dynamics.

Angular velocity is the integral over time of angular acceleration. The angular position of the pendulum is the integral over time of its angular velocity.
