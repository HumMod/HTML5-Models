Overview

High glucose concentration in the extratracellular fluid stimulates insulin release by the pancreas. Insulin, in turn, stimulates cellular uptake of glucose. Low glucose concentration inhibits insulin secretion and stimulates hepatic release of glucose.

This model is scaled to a 70 kG adult.

The independent variable is time in minutes.

The quantities of glucose and insulin in the extracellular fluid volume are the integrals over time of their net rate of change. Glucose concentration is reported in G/dL.

We have a glucose pump and an insulin pump. Each has a switch and setting. When the switch is on, the infusion rate is equal to the setting.
